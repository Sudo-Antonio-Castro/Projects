Processes, Services and Job Scheduling

Process inspection, priorities, cron, at, and service behavior.

Process control

  • Use ps, top, pgrep, and related tools to inspect process state.
  • Job control matters for foreground and background shell work.
  • Signals provide orderly or forceful process termination.

Services and units

  • systemd service files define how processes are started and managed.
  • Directives such as User= help prevent services from running with unnecessary privileges.
  • Service ordering and startup behavior should match operational requirements.

Task scheduling

  • cron manages recurring jobs, while at handles one-time execution.
  • anacron helps systems that are not always running.
  • Accurate system time is essential for logs, automation, and scheduled work.
Quick takeaway

Use this module to connect commands to operational intent. Linux+ rewards knowing which tool to reach for first and why.
