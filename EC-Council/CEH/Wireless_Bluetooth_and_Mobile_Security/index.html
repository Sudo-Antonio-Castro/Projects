Lesson 1: Wi-Fi and Bluetooth

Radio-based exposure

Wireless technologies introduce discovery, interception, rogue infrastructure, and proximity-based attacks.

Focus points

  • Wi-Fi assessments may involve hidden SSIDs, rogue AP detection, encryption analysis, and packet capture.
  • Bluetooth attacks include bluejacking, bluesnarfing, bluebugging, and related discovery techniques.
  • Planning, placement, and monitoring all matter in wireless defense.

Summary

Wireless weakness is often a mix of radio exposure, poor configuration, and weak authentication choices.

Exam angle

If the scenario involves rogue APs or DHCP responses over wireless, think rogue infrastructure detection.

Lesson 2: Mobile Devices

Portable endpoints with layered risk

Mobile devices combine user risk, app risk, OS risk, and network risk in a portable form factor.

Focus points

  • Mobile attacks may target operating system weaknesses, unsafe apps, or insecure connectivity.
  • Hardening includes device encryption, screen locks, update discipline, and management controls.
  • Mobile device management helps enforce policy and visibility at scale.

Summary

Mobile defense must account for loss, theft, user behavior, and application trust all at once.

Exam angle

Questions about centralized mobile control often point to MDM capabilities.

Module focus

Wi-Fi attacks, Bluetooth abuse, and mobile-device compromise paths.

Why it matters

Short-range and mobile technologies widen the attack surface far beyond the wired LAN.
