Lesson 1: Cloud

Remote platforms and shared responsibility

Cloud environments shift how systems are deployed, exposed, and secured.

Focus points

  • Cloud threats may involve weak configuration, exposed data, identity misuse, or service abuse.
  • Security responsibility is shared between provider and customer depending on the service model.
  • Encryption, access control, and visibility remain central in cloud protection.

Summary

Many cloud incidents are still configuration and identity problems rather than magic new categories of failure.

Exam angle

If the question asks who secures what in cloud, think shared responsibility.

Lesson 2: IoT

Small devices, large exposure

IoT devices are often numerous, difficult to patch, and deployed with limited oversight.

Focus points

  • Search and scan tools can identify exposed smart devices on networks or the public internet.
  • IoT protocols and deployment choices may reveal weak authentication or management gaps.
  • Defenders need inventory, segmentation, updates, and least-privilege management.

Summary

IoT security is hard because scale, heterogeneity, and default insecurity often collide.

Exam angle

When the question involves internet-exposed smart devices, discovery platforms like Shodan are often relevant.

Module focus

Shared-responsibility risks, cloud threats, and exposed embedded systems.

Why it matters

The modern attack surface includes services you do not rack yourself and devices you may barely control.
